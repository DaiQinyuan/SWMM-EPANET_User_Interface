◆ surface_layer_storage_depth

core.swmm.hydrology.lidcontrol.LIDControl.surface_layer_storage_depth

When confining walls or berms are present this is the maximum depth to which water can pond above the surface of the unit before overflow occurs (in inches or mm).

For LIDs that experience overland flow it is the height of any surface depression storage. For swales, it is the height of its trapezoidal cross section.
